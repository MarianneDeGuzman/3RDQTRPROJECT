HOW TO PREVENT FIRE IN YOUR HOME
  • Fire Drills
  • Contact Us
1. While cooking, never leave the kitchen unattended.The time it takes for a fire to start is very short. Never leave the stove or oven if you are not watching it. Ask someone to watch the food for you if you have to leave the kitchen for any reason at all, or simply turn everything off until you return.
2. Broken wires should be replaced as soon as possible because they can pose a risk for a fire. Buy new ones at stores. Additionally, keep in mind that when in use, wires can get hot, so pay attention to where you place them and avoid passing them through carpets or other potentially flammable materials.
3. Your best line of defense in the event of a fire starting will be blankets and fire extinguishers. You can buy these at fire equipment stores. So you can put out a fire as quickly as possible if it does start in your home. Fire blankets are also excellent for extinguishing small fires before they grow larger and more fierce.
4. Never leave items near a fire. If exposed to a strong source of heat, household cleaners and products like hair spray or shaving cream may become very dangerous. Always keep these safely locked up and away from heaters in a cool location.
